Home

I am Dr. Arindam Karmakar, an Assistant Professor in Physics at Surya Sen Mahavidyalaya, affiliated with the University of North Bengal, India. My research focuses on experimental condensed matter physics, particularly the magnetic, dielectric, and structural properties of quantum materials, transition metal oxides, and multiferroics. With expertise in synchrotron X-ray and neutron diffraction techniques, I explore the interplay between electronic, magnetic, and structural properties in functional materials. I have been involved in multiple international research collaborations and have conducted experiments at leading synchrotron and neutron facilities. My work spans charge transport, dielectric relaxation, magnetoelectric coupling, and multicaloric effects, contributing to the fundamental understanding and technological advancement of novel materials.

Research Overview

Our research explores the magnetic, dielectric, and structural behavior of transition metal and rare-earth oxides, with a focus on unlocking their potential for advanced technologies. By combining cutting-edge techniques—including neutron diffraction, synchrotron X-ray spectroscopy, and dielectric measurements—we investigate multiferroic phenomena, thermal effects, and charge transport to uncover how atomic-scale distortions and electronic interactions govern material performance. Advanced computational tools (FullProf, JANA, Demeter) enhance our analysis of experimental data, revealing precise structure-property relationships. This integrated approach drives the development of next-generation functional materials for energy-efficient systems, smart electronics, and multifunctional devices, bridging fundamental science with real-world innovation.
