Research Overview

My research focuses on the synthesis, crystal growth and comprehensive characterization of nonlinear optical (NLO) materials, functional oxides and magnetoelectric systems. I investigate the structural, optical, thermal and mechanical properties of semiorganic and hybrid materials to enhance their potential for photonic applications. Additionally, I explore charge transport mechanisms, dielectric relaxation and magnetoelectric coupling in transition metal oxides, particularly cobaltites and ferrites. Through advanced experimental techniques, including synchrotron-based X-ray diffraction and dielectric spectroscopy, I aim to establish structure-property correlations that contribute to the development of novel materials for optoelectronic and energy applications.

Current Research Interests

  • Experimental Condensed Matter Physics with fundamental interests in Crystal Growth, Non-linear optical materials, magnetic and dielectric properties of Quantum magnetic materials, Transition metal oxides, Multiferroics, Electrocaloric and magnetocaloric materials.
  • Materials with applications in battery electrodes
  • Low temperature physics of materials
