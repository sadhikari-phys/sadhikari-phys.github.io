Research Overview

Our research explores the magnetic, dielectric, and structural behavior of transition metal and rare-earth oxides, with a focus on unlocking their potential for advanced technologies. By combining cutting-edge techniques—including neutron diffraction, synchrotron X-ray spectroscopy, and dielectric measurements—we investigate multiferroic phenomena, thermal effects, and charge transport to uncover how atomic-scale distortions and electronic interactions govern material performance. Advanced computational tools (FullProf, JANA, Demeter) enhance our analysis of experimental data, revealing precise structure-property relationships. This integrated approach drives the development of next-generation functional materials for energy-efficient systems, smart electronics, and multifunctional devices, bridging fundamental science with real-world innovation.

Current Research Interests

  • Materials with applications in battery electrodes
  • Low temperature physics of materials
